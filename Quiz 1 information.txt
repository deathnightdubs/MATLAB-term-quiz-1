Dear students,

This announcement is to clarify the structure, expectations, and permitted materials for Quiz 1.

Date and Duration
Quiz 1 will take place on Tuesday, June 16, 2026, at 4:00 PM. The duration of the quiz is 1 hour and 20 minutes.

Quiz Format
Quiz 1 is a written, paper-based quiz. It will include MATLAB short-answer questions, code-reading questions, output-prediction questions, indexing questions, and code-completion questions.

The quiz is based on the MATLAB concepts that we have covered in lectures, tutorials, labs, homework, and posted course materials.

Permitted Materials
Students may not use calculators, phones, laptops, electronic devices, notes, textbooks, or online resources during the quiz.

There will not be a separate reference sheet for Quiz 1. Any information that is necessary for a specific question will be provided directly within that question.

Topics to Review
Students should review the MATLAB concepts covered in the course, including:

    MATLAB variable names and basic syntax,

    logical expressions and logical operators,

    determining the value and class of MATLAB expressions,

    basic MATLAB functions covered in class,

    formatted printing using fprintf,

    vectors and the colon operator,

    row vectors, column vectors, transpose, and concatenation,

    vector and matrix indexing,

    replacing selected vector or matrix elements,

    identifying MATLAB statements that produce an error,

    basic matrix operations and solving simple systems of equations,

    logical operations, conditional indexing, and selecting data based on conditions,

    sorting and filtering data,

    string arrays, string comparisons, and basic string operations,

    simple environmental and geological engineering examples similar to those practiced in class, tutorials, labs, and homework.

This list is intended to help students focus their review. Students should not expect the quiz to provide a separate list of MATLAB commands, definitions, or explanations.

During the Quiz
Please read each question carefully before answering. Show your work clearly where required, and write your final answers in the spaces provided.

If a MATLAB statement produces an error, write ERROR.

If you have a question during the quiz, please raise your hand quietly and wait for the instructor or a TA to come to you. The teaching team may clarify the wording of a question, but cannot answer “how do I solve this?” or debugging-style questions.

If something remains unclear, make a reasonable assumption, write your assumption on the quiz booklet, and complete the question. Your assumption will be considered during marking.

When You Arrive
Please find a seat with a quiz booklet, do not move the quiz booklet to a different seat, and do not open the quiz until instructed to do so.

Please check that you have only one booklet in front of you. Take out a writing utensil and fill in the front page before the quiz begins.

All phones, laptops, calculators, notes, textbooks, and other materials must be put away and stored under your desk.

Please do not talk to other students during the quiz.
